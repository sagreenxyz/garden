## About This Path

The **Basic Nursing Skills Learning Path** delivers the ten clinical modules of D442 in the recommended instructional sequence. Each module builds on foundational concepts established in earlier modules — for example, infection control principles introduced in Section 3 are applied directly in urinary catheterization (Section 9) and wound culture collection (Section 10). Students who complete the modules in order will develop a coherent, layered understanding of fundamental nursing practice that prepares them for the Pre-Lab HESI, the Shadow Health Ticket to Ride DCE, and the On Ground Lab competency assessment.

**Successful completion of all modules and the associated lab assessments is required to progress to Adult Health 1 clinical.**

## What You'll Learn

Working through this path, students will develop knowledge and skills across ten foundational content areas:

- **Health Assessment** — systematic head-to-toe assessment, health history, older adult considerations, and vital signs interpretation
- **Nursing Process and Clinical Judgment** — ADPIE, NANDA-I nursing diagnoses, and the NCSBN Clinical Judgment Measurement Model
- **Infection Control** — chain of infection, standard and transmission-based precautions, PPE donning and doffing, and sterile technique
- **Personal Hygiene** — bed bathing, oral care, skin integrity assessment, and evidence-based pressure injury prevention
- **Mobility** — safe patient handling, transfer techniques, therapeutic positioning, range-of-motion exercises, and fall prevention
- **Nutrition and Tubes** — nutritional screening, nasogastric tube management, enteral feeding administration, and complication prevention
- **Oxygenation** — respiratory assessment, oxygen delivery systems, incentive spirometry, suctioning, and positioning for ventilation
- **Medication Administration** — the six rights, administration routes, high-alert medications, medication reconciliation, and error prevention
- **Elimination** — urinary catheterization, CAUTI prevention, incontinence management, bowel care, and ostomy fundamentals
- **Specimen Collection** — blood, urine, stool, sputum, and wound specimens, with labeling, transport, and patient education

## How to Use This Path

Complete all modules in the order listed above. Each module includes:

- Learning objectives aligned to Bloom's taxonomy
- Clinical content with key concepts, tables, and procedural technique guidance
- NCLEX-NG style practice questions with correct answers and rationales
- Standards alignment references (AACN Essentials, NCLEX-NG CJMM, QSEN, CCNE, ACEN)

After completing the didactic modules, proceed to the corresponding Sherpath adaptive learning activities, Shadow Health simulations, and Clinical Skills procedural videos to reinforce and practice the skills in preparation for the On Ground Lab assessment.
