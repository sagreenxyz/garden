## Overview

Azithromycin (Z-Pack) is a macrolide antibiotic with a unique pharmacokinetic profile — it concentrates intracellularly and has a long tissue half-life, enabling once-daily dosing and a 5-day course. It is the primary antibiotic for atypical CAP pathogens (Mycoplasma, Chlamydophila, Legionella) and carries an FDA cardiac safety warning for QT prolongation.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ **Azithromycin:** QT prolongation risk — check QTc before and during therapy. Covers ATYPICAL CAP pathogens (Mycoplasma, Chlamydophila, Legionella) — must combine with beta-lactam for hospitalized CAP. Long half-life = once-daily dosing, 5-day Z-Pack course. GI side effects are most common macrolide adverse effect. Does NOT require renal dose adjustment (hepatic elimination).
