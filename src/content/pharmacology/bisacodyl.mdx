## Overview

Bisacodyl is a stimulant laxative available in oral and rectal forms. The rectal suppository acts more quickly (15-60 min) than oral tablets (6-12 hours). Enteric-coated tablets must not be crushed or taken with antacids.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ , Bisacodyl tablets: enteric coated — DO NOT crush or take with antacids/milk (destroys coating). Suppository onset: 15-60 min. Oral onset: 6-12 hours. Chronic use = laxative dependence risk.

## Drug Class Memory Aid

**'Bisacodyl tablet = swallow whole, no antacids'** — enteric coating must be intact
