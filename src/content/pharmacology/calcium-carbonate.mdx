## Overview

Calcium carbonate serves both as an antacid and calcium supplement. Unlike calcium citrate, it is best absorbed when taken with meals and can cause constipation.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Calcium carbonate: take WITH meals (food needed for absorption). Causes CONSTIPATION (opposite of magnesium antacids = diarrhea). Interferes with many drug absorptions — space by 2 hours.

## Drug Class Memory Aid

**'Calcium carbonate = constipating antacid taken with food'** — absorbs better with meals
