## Overview

Edoxaban is unique among DOACs in that it is LESS effective than warfarin in AFib patients with very high renal clearance (CrCl > 95 mL/min). This is a NCLEX-relevant fact.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Edoxaban AVOID in CrCl > 95 mL/min for AFib (paradoxically less effective). Antidote = andexanet alfa. Start after 5-10 days parenteral anticoagulation for VTE.

## Drug Class Memory Aid
