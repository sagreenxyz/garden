## Overview

Triamterene blocks sodium channels in the collecting duct independently of aldosterone (unlike spironolactone). It is often combined with HCTZ (Dyazide/Maxzide) to counteract potassium loss.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Triamterene SPARES K+ — do NOT add K+ supplements. Can cause triamterene kidney stones. Monitor K+ and renal function closely.

## Drug Class Memory Aid

**'Triamterene = K+ sparing sodium channel blocker'** — independent of aldosterone, unlike spironolactone
