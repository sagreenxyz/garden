## Overview

Betamethasone is given to mothers before premature delivery to accelerate fetal lung maturation and reduce neonatal respiratory distress syndrome. It is given as two IM doses 24 hours apart at 24-34 weeks gestation.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Antenatal betamethasone: given to MOTHER (not baby). Two IM doses 24 hours apart at 24-34 weeks. Reduces neonatal RDS, IVH, and mortality. Monitor maternal blood glucose — hyperglycemia expected.

## Drug Class Memory Aid

**'Betamethasone in pregnancy = fetal lung maturity'** — given to mom before 34 weeks; reduces baby's RDS
