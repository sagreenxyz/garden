## Overview

Ticlopidine was the first P2Y12 inhibitor but is largely abandoned due to serious hematologic toxicity (agranulocytosis, TTP). It has been replaced by clopidogrel and newer agents.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Ticlopidine: serious risk of AGRANULOCYTOSIS (2-3%) and TTP. Monitor CBC every 2 weeks for 3 months. Largely replaced by safer alternatives. Historically significant as first P2Y12 inhibitor.

## Drug Class Memory Aid

**'Ticlopidine = toxic thienopyridine'** — replaced by clopidogrel; agranulocytosis and TTP risk
