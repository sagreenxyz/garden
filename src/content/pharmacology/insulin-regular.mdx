## Overview

Regular insulin is a short-acting insulin and the ONLY insulin formulation that can be given intravenously. It is used in DKA management, IV glucose control in critical illness, and SQ with meals.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Regular insulin: ONLY insulin given IV. IV infusion: binds to tubing — flush 50 mL first. Onset: 30-60 min SQ. Give 30 min before meals. Hypoglycemia antidote: glucagon or dextrose. Clear, colorless appearance.
