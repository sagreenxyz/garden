## Overview

Glipizide is a second-generation sulfonylurea that stimulates insulin secretion. It causes hypoglycemia, especially in the elderly and those with irregular meal schedules. Immediate-release is taken 30 minutes before meals.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Sulfonylureas (glipizide, glyburide): cause HYPOGLYCEMIA — give 30 min before meals (IR). Most dangerous in elderly and fasting. Avoid with sulfonamide allergy. Take 30 min before first meal.

## Drug Class Memory Aid

**'Sulfonylureas = hypoglycemia risk'** — take before meals; most dangerous in elderly with irregular eating
