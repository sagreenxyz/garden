## Overview

Magnesium hydroxide is both an antacid and osmotic laxative. It is contraindicated in renal failure due to risk of magnesium accumulation and hypermagnesemia toxicity.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Magnesium hydroxide: AVOID in renal failure — hypermagnesemia risk. Signs of Mg++ toxicity: absent reflexes → respiratory depression → cardiac arrest. Reduces absorption of other drugs — space by 2 hours.

## Drug Class Memory Aid

**'Mg(OH)2 + renal failure = hypermagnesemia'** — absent reflexes = toxicity warning sign
