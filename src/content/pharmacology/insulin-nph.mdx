## Overview

NPH is an intermediate-acting insulin that appears cloudy due to its protamine suspension. It must be gently rolled (not shaken) before use. When mixing with regular insulin: draw clear before cloudy.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ NPH insulin: CLOUDY — roll gently, don't shake. Draw CLEAR (regular) before CLOUDY (NPH) when mixing. Peak 4-12 hours (highest hypoglycemia risk). Do NOT give IV. Give twice daily typically.

## Drug Class Memory Aid

**'Clear before Cloudy'** — draw regular (clear) before NPH (cloudy) when mixing insulins
