## Overview

Heparin is a rapidly acting parenteral anticoagulant monitored by aPTT. HIT is a life-threatening immune complication causing paradoxical thrombosis. Treatment of HIT requires stopping heparin and using an alternative anticoagulant.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ HEPARIN antidote = PROTAMINE SULFATE. Monitor aPTT (goal: 60-100 sec). Monitor PLATELETS for HIT (drop > 50% = suspect HIT). Do NOT give IM. HIT = stop heparin immediately, use argatroban or bivalirudin.

## Drug Class Memory Aid

**'HIT = Heparin-Induced Thrombocytopenia'** — platelet DROP causes CLOTS (paradoxical). Stop heparin NOW.
