## Overview

Ketorolac is the most potent parenteral NSAID used for short-term acute pain management. It must not be used for more than 5 days (combined oral and parenteral) due to high risk of GI bleeding.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ , Ketorolac: MAXIMUM 5 DAYS (combined IV + oral). Highest GI toxicity of common NSAIDs. Useful as opioid-sparing therapy in acute pain. Avoid in renal failure and elderly.

## Drug Class Memory Aid

**'Ketorolac = 5-day maximum'** — highest GI toxicity risk; more than 5 days is contraindicated
