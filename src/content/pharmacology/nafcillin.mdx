## Overview

Nafcillin is hepatically eliminated (unlike other penicillins which are renally cleared), making it useful in patients with renal failure requiring MSSA therapy. It is highly irritating to peripheral veins.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Nafcillin: HEPATICALLY cleared — no renal dose adjustment. Very vein-irritating — prefer PICC/central line. Monitor CBC for neutropenia with prolonged use. NOT effective against MRSA.

## Drug Class Memory Aid

**'Nafcillin = liver cleared, vein irritant'** — no renal dose adjustment; needs central access for prolonged use
