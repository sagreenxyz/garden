## Overview

Metoprolol is a cardioselective beta-1 blocker widely used for hypertension, angina, and heart failure. Its selectivity for beta-1 receptors makes it safer than non-selective beta blockers in patients with reactive airway disease.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Always assess the apical pulse for a full minute before giving any beta blocker. Hold and notify provider if HR < 60. Never abruptly stop — risk of rebound angina or MI. In diabetics, masks tachycardia but NOT diaphoresis as a hypoglycemia sign.

## Drug Class Memory Aid

**'Beta blockers BLOCK the BEAT'**: ↓ Heart Rate, ↓ Contractility, ↓ Blood Pressure, ↓ Renin release
