## Overview

Ceftriaxone (Rocephin) is the most widely used third-generation cephalosporin with once-daily dosing, excellent CNS penetration, and broad gram-negative coverage. It is a cornerstone antibiotic for hospitalized CAP (paired with azithromycin), bacterial meningitis (with vancomycin), and gonorrhea (IM single dose).

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ **Ceftriaxone:** NEVER mix with calcium-containing IV solutions (e.g., Ringer's lactate, calcium infusions) — precipitation risk, fatal in neonates. First-line for bacterial MENINGITIS (with vancomycin) — excellent CNS penetration. Drug of choice for GONORRHEA (IM single dose 500 mg). Does NOT cover Pseudomonas aeruginosa — use cefepime if Pseudomonas is suspected. Biliary sludging with prolonged use — monitor for right upper quadrant pain.
