## Overview

Tetracycline is the prototype drug of its class. Key distinctions: must be taken on an empty stomach (unlike doxycycline), and expired tetracycline causes Fanconi syndrome (proximal tubular dysfunction).

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Tetracycline: EMPTY STOMACH (unlike doxycycline — less food effect). Never take EXPIRED tetracycline — causes Fanconi syndrome. Chelates with dairy, antacids, iron. Avoid in children < 8 and pregnancy.

## Drug Class Memory Aid

**'Expired tetracycline = Fanconi syndrome'** — always check expiration date; take on empty stomach
