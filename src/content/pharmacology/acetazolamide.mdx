## Overview

Acetazolamide inhibits carbonic anhydrase, reducing bicarbonate reabsorption. Its main clinical uses are glaucoma, altitude sickness prevention, and metabolic alkalosis correction.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Acetazolamide causes metabolic ACIDOSIS (loses bicarbonate) and hyperchloremic acidosis. Paresthesias are expected. Start before altitude ascent. Contraindicated in sulfonamide allergy.

## Drug Class Memory Aid

**'Acetazolamide = ACID-osis'** — loses bicarbonate → metabolic acidosis; tingling is expected
