## Overview

Amlodipine is a dihydropyridine CCB that selectively dilates peripheral arteries. It has a very long half-life (~35-50 hours) allowing once-daily dosing and gradual onset.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Amlodipine most common side effect = peripheral edema (ankle swelling). Long half-life — takes 7–8 days to reach steady state. No negative inotropic effect — safe in heart failure (PRAISE trial).

## Drug Class Memory Aid

**'Amlodipine = Ankle edema'** — most common side effect is peripheral (dependent) edema
