## Overview

Acyclovir is the prototype antiviral for herpesvirus infections. IV administration requires slow infusion (1 hour) and adequate hydration to prevent crystalluria and nephrotoxicity.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Acyclovir IV: give over 60 MINUTES with adequate hydration (prevent renal crystalluria). Dose adjust in renal failure. Does NOT cure herpes — suppresses outbreaks. Valacyclovir preferred orally (better bioavailability).

## Drug Class Memory Aid

**'Acyclovir IV = slow infusion + hydration'** — crystalluria and nephrotoxicity with rapid infusion
