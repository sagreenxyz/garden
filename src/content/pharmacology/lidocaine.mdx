## Overview

Lidocaine is both a cardiac antiarrhythmic and the prototype local anesthetic. IV lidocaine treats ventricular arrhythmias; its CNS toxicity manifests progressively from sedation to seizures to cardiac arrest.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ , IV lidocaine CNS toxicity progression: ringing in ears → drowsiness → confusion → SEIZURES → cardiac arrest. NOT effective orally (first-pass metabolism). Reduce dose in liver disease.
