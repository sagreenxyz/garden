## Overview

Ibuprofen is the most widely used NSAID with analgesic, antipyretic, and anti-inflammatory effects. Long-term use carries risks of GI bleeding, renal impairment, and cardiovascular events.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ NSAIDs: AVOID in GI ulcers, renal failure, third trimester pregnancy, post-CABG. Take WITH FOOD. Reduce antihypertensive effectiveness. Monitor K+ and renal function with long-term use.

## Drug Class Memory Aid

**'NSAIDs = GI protection needed'** — take with food; avoid in renal failure, third trimester, post-CABG
