## Overview

Enoxaparin is the most commonly used LMWH with more predictable pharmacokinetics than UFH, usually not requiring routine monitoring. Anti-Xa levels are checked in special populations.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Enoxaparin in RENAL failure — accumulates, REDUCE DOSE or switch to UFH if CrCl < 30. Inject in ABDOMEN — do not aspirate or rub. Antidote: protamine (partial). Monitor platelets for HIT (less risk than UFH).
