## Overview

Methimazole is the preferred antithyroid drug except in the first trimester of pregnancy (where propylthiouracil is preferred). Agranulocytosis is the most serious adverse effect — requires immediate evaluation if fever/sore throat occurs.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Methimazole: AVOID in first trimester (aplasia cutis). Agranulocytosis risk — stop immediately for fever/sore throat, check WBC. Preferred antithyroid AFTER first trimester. Takes weeks to work.
