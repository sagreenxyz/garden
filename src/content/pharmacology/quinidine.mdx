## Overview

Quinidine was the first antiarrhythmic drug. Its use is limited due to cinchonism, QT prolongation (quinidine syncope), and drug interactions (increases digoxin levels by 50%). Still used for malaria as IV quinidine gluconate.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Quinidine toxicity = CINCHONISM (tinnitus + headache + visual changes + diarrhea). INCREASES digoxin levels — reduce digoxin 50%. Torsades de pointes (quinidine syncope). Largely replaced by safer antiarrhythmics.

## Drug Class Memory Aid

**'Quinidine = Cinchonism + increases digoxin levels'** — tinnitus is early toxicity; QT prolongation
