## Overview

Citalopram has dose-dependent QT prolongation, limiting maximum doses. Escitalopram (the S-enantiomer) is preferred for lower side effect profile and fewer drug interactions.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Citalopram: QT prolongation risk — max 40 mg/day (20 mg if elderly > 60 or liver disease). Escitalopram is the preferred enantiomer. 14-day MAOI washout. Standard SSRI monitoring.

## Drug Class Memory Aid

**'Citalopram = QT dose limit'** — max 40 mg (20 mg elderly); escitalopram is the better S-isomer
