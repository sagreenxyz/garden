## Overview

Metoclopramide is the most widely used GI prokinetic for gastroparesis. Its use is limited to 12 weeks due to risk of tardive dyskinesia — irreversible involuntary movements — which carries a Black Box Warning.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Metoclopramide: BLACK BOX WARNING — tardive dyskinesia (irreversible) with use > 12 weeks. EPS treated with diphenhydramine or benztropine. Blocks dopamine — also an antiemetic (CTZ blockade).
