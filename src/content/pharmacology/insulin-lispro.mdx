## Overview

Insulin lispro is a rapid-acting insulin analog that can be given 0-15 minutes before meals, making it more convenient than regular insulin (which requires 30 minutes). It is also used in insulin pumps.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Insulin lispro: onset 15-30 min — give 0-15 MINUTES before meals (vs. regular: 30 min before). Peak 0.5-1.5 hours. Can give after meal if food intake uncertain. Rapid-acting: lispro = Humalog.

## Drug Class Memory Aid

**'Rapid-acting insulin = give within 15 min of eating'** — much faster than regular insulin (30 min)
