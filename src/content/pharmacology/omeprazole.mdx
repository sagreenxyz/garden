## Overview

Omeprazole is the prototype PPI, irreversibly inhibiting the proton pump. It requires activation in an acidic environment, so it must be taken before meals. Long-term use carries risks of magnesium deficiency and C. difficile.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ , Take 30-60 MINUTES BEFORE meals (must be activated by acid — needs to eat). Long-term risks: hypomagnesemia, B12 deficiency, C. diff risk, fractures. Avoid with clopidogrel (reduces efficacy by 40%).
