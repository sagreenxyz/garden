## Overview

Sucralfate forms a protective gel barrier over ulcer craters in acidic conditions. It must be taken on an empty stomach before meals and must NOT be combined with antacids (which raise pH, preventing activation).

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Sucralfate: take on EMPTY STOMACH, before meals. Antacids INACTIVATE it — separate by 30 min. Binds many drugs — separate by 2 hours. Needs ACIDIC environment to work.

## Drug Class Memory Aid

**'Sucralfate = empty stomach + no antacids'** — needs acidic pH to activate and form protective gel
