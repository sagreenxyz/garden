## Overview

Ofloxacin is available in oral, ophthalmic, and otic preparations. The otic formulation must be warmed before instillation — cold drops can cause vertigo and dizziness.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Ofloxacin otic: WARM before instilling (cold = dizziness/vertigo). Tilt head, remain 5 minutes. Ophthalmic: do not touch dropper tip to eye. Systemic form: same fluoroquinolone Black Box Warnings.

## Drug Class Memory Aid

**'Warm otic drops before use'** — cold drops cause vertigo; applies to all otic medications
