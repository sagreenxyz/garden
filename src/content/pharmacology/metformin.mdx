## Overview

Metformin is the first-line oral antidiabetic for Type 2 diabetes. It does not cause hypoglycemia when used as monotherapy. Lactic acidosis is a rare but serious risk, primarily in renal failure.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Metformin: HOLD for contrast dye + 48 hours (lactic acidosis risk). CONTRAINDICATED GFR < 30. Does NOT cause hypoglycemia as monotherapy. Start low, titrate slowly for GI effects. Monitor B12 long-term.
