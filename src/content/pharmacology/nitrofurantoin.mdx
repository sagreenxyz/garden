## Overview

Nitrofurantoin is a urinary antiseptic that concentrates exclusively in urine, making it appropriate only for UTI treatment and prophylaxis. It is ineffective (and potentially harmful) when GFR < 30.

## NCLEX High-Yield Points

> ⚠️ Nitrofurantoin: ONLY for UTI (concentrated in urine only). AVOID when GFR < 30 (not effective and accumulates). Brown urine — harmless. Monitor for pulmonary toxicity (cough/dyspnea) with prolonged use.

## Drug Class Memory Aid

**'Nitrofurantoin = UTI only, avoid in renal failure'** — concentrated in urine; brown urine is normal
